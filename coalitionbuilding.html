Coalition Building

The American Citizens for Justice hold a banner with the words "It's Not Fair," Vincent Chin's last words.

Coalition building was a crucial part for the movement for justice for Vincent Chin. It allowed them to have more power with more people. The first important step was creating the American Citizens for Justice (ACJ). This gave the movement a centralized organization to create decisions from and move forward with a unified strategy. Starting with the many Asian American community leaders, including those from Detroit Chinese Welfare Council, On Leong Merchants Association, Detroit Chinese Engineering Association, Chinese American Educational and Cultural Center, and womenâ€™s groups, church organizations, and more. With the formation of the ACJ, they intentionally chose the name to declare Asians as American and to be broad enough to create pan-Asian solidarity and further Asian American advocacy. The formation of a centralized organization allowed for a unified approach, further solidarity building, and more numbers that were important to gain more power. It was also the center to mobilize and organize supporters, assembling demonstrations and more to build a coordinated movement for justice. This united organization was the starting point for future steps to build the Asian American movement, starting with the case around Vincent Chin.
